Profile

Cross-disciplinary, by design

I am a Ph.D.-trained computational engineer (NTU, 2020) and a Security by Design master's candidate at SUTD, working on privacy-preserving AI, homomorphic encryption and secure AI systems for RAG, encrypted inference and critical-infrastructure applications.

My path is deliberately cross-disciplinary. A decade in mathematical modelling, numerical computation and critical-infrastructure analytics — including years building a 24/7 AI digital twin for Singapore's national water network — taught me what it takes for analytics to run in production on sensitive data. My current research turns that lesson into cryptography: stage-aware CKKS/TFHE workflows, confidential retrieval and reranking, and encrypted time-series analytics, with two first-authored papers accepted at ACNS 2026. The through-line is an AI-safety one — building confidential-AI mechanisms that let models assist real decisions while reducing exposure of the prompts, retrieved documents, embeddings and sensitive telemetry they depend on.

Research Positioning

Stage-aware encrypted AI for confidential RAG and time-series

Core objective: build and evaluate stage-aware encrypted AI pipelines for RAG and time-series inference — using CKKS where approximate vector arithmetic is suitable, and TFHE where low-bit, Boolean or control-heavy operations are more natural.

CKKS / TFHE co-design

The right scheme per stage

CKKS for approximate encrypted vector operations — similarity, aggregation, time-series features — and TFHE for Boolean, integer and control-heavy components, with explicit measurement of ciphertext-conversion costs at the boundary.

Confidential RAG

Protecting the whole pipeline

Reducing data- and model-exposure risk: privacy-preserving mechanisms that keep prompts, retrieved context, system instructions, embeddings, reranking signals and intermediate states protected across retrieval, scoring and generation support.

HE systems & measurement

Making it measurable

HE-friendly operator placement, ciphertext packing, noise and precision budgeting, bootstrapping-aware evaluation, and latency/memory benchmarking grounded in empirical prototype measurement.

Applied & high-trust

Where confidentiality is operational

Critical-infrastructure AI, water-utility digital twins and IoT/telemetry analytics — high-trust workflows where confidentiality, correctness and auditability are operational safety requirements.

Relevant research directions

stage-aware RAG

Map query intake, embedding, retrieval, scoring, reranking, context assembly and generation-support stages by arithmetic precision, control flow and ciphertext-conversion cost.

hybrid CKKS/TFHE

Leverage CKKS for approximate encrypted vector operations and TFHE for Boolean/integer/control-heavy components, with explicit measurement of conversion costs across the boundary.

privacy & safety

Specify which artifacts must be protected — prompts, retrieved documents, system instructions, metadata, embeddings, reranking outputs and sensitive telemetry — and tie technical guarantees to leakage and governance risk.

encrypted time-series

Extend TTNeural and smart-water-grid experience to measure encrypted inference under latency, accuracy, memory and reliability constraints on critical-infrastructure data.

reproducible benchmarking

Generate prototype measurements and clear trade-off summaries that translate HE performance and security constraints into practical confidential-AI deployment evidence.
